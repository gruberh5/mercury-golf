# Product Principles

## 1. Mercury Informs Decisions

Mercury should recommend and explain. It should not remove player judgment.

## 2. Learn From Data

Mercury should learn from actual results whenever possible instead of relying only on hardcoded assumptions.

## 3. Optimize For Lower Scores

The goal is not prettier statistics. The goal is better scoring decisions.

## 4. Fast During Play

On-course recommendations must be quick. A golfer cannot wait 30 seconds over the ball.

## 5. Simple During Play, Detailed After Play

The on-course interface should be minimal.

The post-round interface can be deeper and more analytical.

## 6. Separate Decision, Execution, And Outcome

A good decision can have a bad result.

A bad decision can get lucky.

Mercury should track these separately:

- Decision quality
- Execution quality
- Outcome quality

## 7. Confidence Matters

Golf decisions are not purely mathematical. Confidence, trust, and shot comfort should be part of Mercury's recommendations.

## 8. Explain The Recommendation

Every recommendation should include a short reason.

Example:

> Target center green. Water short makes the front pin too costly.
