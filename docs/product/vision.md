# Product Vision

Mercury is a personal golf intelligence platform built to help one player make better golf decisions.

Mercury is a self-built alternative to premium golf GPS and stat tracking apps, designed around one golfer instead of a generic user base.

It should eventually replace the need for apps like 18Birdies during a round while adding deeper personal analytics and an AI caddie layer.

Mercury is not a swing coach or launch monitor. It is a golf decision intelligence system.

## Purpose

Mercury helps answer questions like:

- What is the yardage to the front, middle, and back of the green?
- How far did the last shot actually travel?
- What club fits this exact lie, wind, target, and hazard situation?
- Which decisions are costing strokes?
- Which clubs produce the best scoring outcomes?
- Is mini driver actually outperforming driver?
- Does rough change shot shape or miss tendency?
- Did aggressive pin hunting help or hurt?
- Which equipment changes improve scoring?

## Product Thesis

Golf scores are influenced by more than swing quality.

They are also influenced by:

- Yardage precision
- Course layout
- Hole strategy
- GPS position
- Club selection
- Target selection
- Risk tolerance
- Course management
- Equipment setup
- Weather
- Lie
- Confidence
- Historical tendencies

Mercury should help connect those inputs to better decisions.

## Product Pillars

### 1. GPS And Course Intelligence

Mercury should provide on-course yardages, hole context, course maps, target distances, and shot location tracking.

### 2. Score And Stat Tracking

Mercury should track the round in enough detail to support meaningful post-round analysis.

### 3. Shot And Club Tracking

Mercury should use GPS-assisted shot locations to estimate how far clubs actually travel on the course.

### 4. AI Caddie

Mercury should recommend clubs, targets, and risk levels using the player's real profile, bag setup, ball, tendencies, misses, confidence, course context, and weather.

### 5. Post-Round Analytics

Mercury should use stored data to analyze strokes gained, equipment performance, decision quality, and scoring patterns.

## What Mercury Should Feel Like

During play, Mercury should feel fast, calm, and simple.

After play, Mercury should feel analytical, specific, and honest.

It should give useful information without pretending to be certain about things it cannot know.

## Decision Boundary

Mercury informs decisions. The golfer remains the final authority.

The product should avoid language that sounds like it is making the decision for the player. It should recommend, explain, and quantify risk.
